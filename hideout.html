Hideout Coffee Company


Context For this project, a black and white version of the logo was provided and I was only allowed to use two spot colors, as well as the majority of the copy included on the materials.
Concept The first step was to establish a brand identity for Hideout Coffee Company. I started with a brainstorming session focused on the word ‘hideout’ to see what type of word associations could possibly lend themselves to a compelling visual direction. Ultimately, the rustic, return-to-nature brand identity of Hideout Coffee Company was inspired by both the slab serif typeface in the provided logo and the sense of ‘hiding out’ one feels when they retreat to a mellow mountain town. The logo provided included a slab serif typeface, reminiscent of old American Western woodblock lettering, which led me to think of Roslyn, Washington—a small town nestled in the Cascades that, in the early 1900s, was a bustling center for coal mining. Nowadays, Roslyn is a quaint place to visit for a quick bite on the way to a myriad of outdoor activities in the area. I could easily imagine Hideout Coffee Company located in this sleepy mountain town. After envisioning Hideout Coffee Company in Roslyn, the visuals quickly crystalized in my mind’s eye and then began the process of applying the brand identity across all assets.
