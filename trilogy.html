Trilogy


Context
Trilogy, a gender neutral shampoo, conditioner, and body wash, needed a redesign of their product packaging. For this project, it was important that packaging strike a balance between feminine and masculine, and also crucial that the packaging make clear the 3-in-1 nature of the product.

The target demographic for this product is largely millennials, but the company hopes to begin reaching a broader age range and grow their brand in new markets. The brand aims to be a future competitor for brands such as Lush and Kiehl’s.

Concept
With this information, I began to envision an identity that was both bold and clean. I imagined a product that reflected the diversity of the target audience. I chose a rich blue and a bright orange as the primary colors to ensure that the product would not feel too feminine or masculine. I made the decision to emphasize what the product was above all else because having a 3-in-1 product

I made the decision to emphasize the 3-in-1 product above all other information because this would be the differentiator between Trilogy’s Clean product and most other products on the market.

The patterned design was derived from an idea to illustrate the range of textures and shapes that this product might encounter—short hair, long hair, curly hair, curves, and more. The end pattern is playful yet predictable and aims to feel welcoming to all.

The Old Style typeface Gastromond conveys the bespoke, hand-crafted nature of the Trilogy product line, paired with Mr Eaves XL Modern, a geometric sans-serif typeface with a relatively large x-height and shorter ascenders and descenders, together create an expressive yet accessible voice for the brand.
